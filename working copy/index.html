Andean Cat

Geographic Location: High Andes Mountains
How Many: Fewer than 2500 are left
Habitat: This terrain is arid, sparsely vegetated, rocky and steep
Threats: Loss of habitat, hunting and disease are all contributing factors.

Tiger

Geographic Location: Siberian temperate forests, to subtropical and tropical forests and Sumatra.
How Many: Between 3,062 - 3,948 are left.
Habitat: These forest are tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests as well as, moist evergreen forests, tropical dry forests and swamp forests.
Threats: Loss of habitat, disease, and other predators like crocodiles, bears, and large packs of dholes.

Iberian Lynx

Geographic Location: Sierra Morena and Montes de Toledo of Castilla-La Mancha, Spain, and Portugal.
How Many: Fewer than 1,111
Habitat: Largely restricted to mountainous areas.
Threats: Habitat loss, road accidents, and illegal hunting.

Borneo Bay Cat

Geographic Location: The island of Borneo
How Many: Fewer than 2500 are left
Habitat: Lowland, hill, or swamp forests
Threats: This cat is dependant on forest and therefore threatened by deforestation

Flat Headed Cat

Geographic Location: Thai-Malay Peninsula, Borneo, and Sumatra.
How Many: 2,500 mature individuals, with small subpopulations of no more than 250 adults.
Habitat: Lowland tropical rainforests, freshwater habitats near coastal and lowland areas.
Threats: Wetland and lowland forest destruction and degradation.

About Us

The SECA association aims at fostering research and conservation of endangered cats, as well as, all endangered animals. Our goal is to fully protect these animals with national legislation in all countries that have an animal on the endangered list. We are trying to stop the hunting and trade of these animals as well as support as many wildlife perserves as possible. Our mission is to protect these animals by continued education and preservation.
